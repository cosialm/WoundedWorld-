We Are You

Doctors, teachers, students, parents. United by conscience, driven by love.

Reza Hashemi

Field Coordinator, Tehran

"I watched my clinic collapse. Now I help rebuild others."

Dr. Reza Hashemi was treating patients when the earthquake hit Rafsanjan. His clinic, built with 15 years of savings, crumbled in 30 seconds. Instead of rebuilding for himself, he used his insurance money to buy mobile medical equipment. "If my clinic had to fall," he says, "at least it can rise again wherever it's needed most."

Today, Reza coordinates our medical response across Iran, training local volunteers and establishing pop-up clinics in crisis zones. He carries a photo of his destroyed clinic in his medical bag - not as a reminder of loss, but as proof that from every ending, something new can begin.

Layla Al-Rashid

Community Organizer, Gaza

"They destroyed our schools. So we teach in the rubble."

Layla was a primary school teacher before the bombing. When her school was destroyed, she didn't wait for reconstruction. She gathered the surviving desks, found a partially standing wall, and continued teaching. "Education is resistance," she says. "Every lesson is an act of defiance."

Now she coordinates our education initiatives across Gaza, running mobile schools and distributing learning materials. Her classroom has no roof, but it has something more important: the unbreakable spirit of children who refuse to stop learning.

Amara Johnson

Digital Coordinator, London

"Every share is a voice. Every voice matters."

Amara was a social media manager for a tech company when she saw the first videos from Gaza. "I realized I was using my skills to sell products while people were dying," she recalls. She quit her job the next day and dedicated her expertise to amplifying humanitarian voices.

She now manages our global digital campaigns, turning social media into a tool for justice. Under her leadership, our content has reached over 50 million people, proving that the internet can be a force for compassion, not just commerce.

Hassan Okafor

Logistics Director, Nairobi

"Every box we pack carries someone's tomorrow."

Hassan fled Somalia as a refugee 20 years ago. He knows what it means to depend on the kindness of strangers. "I remember the taste of that first meal in the refugee camp," he says. "It tasted like hope." Now he ensures that hope reaches others.

As our logistics director, Hassan coordinates the movement of supplies across four countries. His warehouse in Nairobi processes 10,000 aid packages monthly. Each one is packed with the care of someone who remembers what it means to be hungry, to be displaced, to be forgotten.
